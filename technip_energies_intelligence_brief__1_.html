Technip Energies is a pure-play engineering & technology business: no upstream exposure, no commodity risk, no asset ownership. Earnings come from EPC for LNG / hydrogen / chemicals plus high-margin Technology Products & Services (TPS) — process tech, proprietary licences, advanced materials. Spin-off from TechnipFMC (Feb 2021) freed both companies to specialise: TechnipFMC retains subsea, T.EN retains onshore process tech.
02

Strategy & Intelligence

LNG dominance via modular technology
  • SnapLNG by T.EN — fully modular, electrified 2.5 Mtpa liquefaction solution. Deployed at Commonwealth LNG Louisiana (6 trains, 9.5 Mtpa).
  • Marsa LNG (Oman) + Ruwais LNG (UAE) — electric-driven motors powered by renewable energy; positioned as among the lowest-carbon-intensity LNG plants globally.
  • 105+ Mtpa installed liquefaction capacity to date — ~20% of global LNG infrastructure (T.EN disclosure).
  • 900 Mtpa liquefaction capacity required by mid-decade per Pieton (Q3 2025 call) — structurally bullish on next-cycle awards.
Energy transition services as second leg
  • Net Zero Teesside Power (UK) — world's first commercial-scale gas-fired power station with carbon capture; designed for 2 Mtpa CO₂ capture. Flagship Canopy by T.EN deployment.
  • BlueH₂ by T.EN — low-carbon H2 with up to 99% CO₂ capture rate. Q1 2025 blue ammonia award in US.
  • Largest green ammonia project globally awarded India 2024 (T.EN execution); Pieton notes "momentum is not a very recurring trend of awards."
  • Rejoule + Ecoplanta — circular economy play (rPET yarn, Spain waste-to-methanol with Repsol).
Pure-play structure creates a hedged bet
  • "We are naturally hedged and positioned to thrive in any energy scenario" — Pieton, Q3 2025.
  • No upstream exposure → unaffected by oil price swings; revenue tracks capex cycle of operators.
  • The transition is a "game of resilience": Pieton's framing favours pragmatism over forced commitment to a single tech.
  • Recent TPS softness — backlog -16% from year start — reflects low-carbon capex pause, not LNG cycle.
Where FutureBridge can engage
  • Carbon capture engineering services scaling — competitive intelligence on Sulzer, Mitsubishi MHI, Aker Carbon Capture, Honeywell UOP.
  • SnapLNG modular references — differentiation vs Bechtel, Saipem, McDermott in next-cycle FOAK awards.
  • Ethylene cycle re-baseline — order intake timing and competitive lock-in (vs Lummus, KBR, ABB).
  • AM&C acquisition integration — diligence on traditional + future ethylene growth markets.

04

Key Initiatives & Progress

Commonwealth LNG (US, Louisiana)
SnapLNG FOAK
6 trains × ~1.6 Mtpa each = 9.5 Mtpa capacity using SnapLNG modular liquefaction. Limited notice to proceed awarded; awaiting full FID. Reference plant for next-cycle SnapLNG sales — if successful, repeats globally.
Net Zero Teesside Power (UK)
CCS flagship
World's first commercial-scale gas-fired power station with CCS, designed for 2 Mtpa CO₂ capture. T.EN delivers Canopy carbon capture (post-combustion, Shell CANSOLV-licensed) and EPC. UK Track-1 cluster anchor.
Coral Norte FLNG (Mozambique)
FID taken
Floating LNG unit; FID achieved, awaiting administrative completion before execution. T.EN lead EPC contractor; second Coral after Coral South delivered 2022.
Rovuma LNG (Mozambique)
FEED stage
FEED executed by T.EN; competing for execution-phase award. Onshore facility ~15+ Mtpa scale; FID expected 2026-2027 dependent on Rovuma security situation.
Marsa LNG (Oman) + Ruwais LNG (UAE)
Execution
Electric-driven liquefaction trains powered by renewable / low-carbon energy; among the lowest carbon-intensity LNG facilities globally. Showcase for SnapLNG decarbonised modular pattern.
India green ammonia (largest globally)
2024 award
Largest green ammonia project worldwide by capacity at award; T.EN selected to execute. Shows green-H₂ economics work at the right scale + geography but Pieton calls out: order momentum has not become recurring.

Weak Signals — FutureBridge Intelligence

S-01
Ethylene cycle re-baseline 2026
Pieton calls out "early signs of recovery" with anticipated 2026 order intake; ethylene 3-4% per annum growth driver. AM&C acquisition positions T.EN for both traditional + future ethylene markets. Timing is the question.
S-02
SnapLNG FOAK risk at Commonwealth
First six-train modular plant globally. Successful execution = template for next-cycle wins. Slippage or cost overrun = setback for the modular thesis.
S-03
Green hydrogen pace softer than expected
Largest global green ammonia award delivered, but Pieton: "momentum is not a very recurring trend of awards." Implies pipeline weaker than 2022-2023 expectations; timing for second commercial wave unclear.
S-04
TPS macro pause
TPS backlog -16% from year start; trending bottom of €1.8-2.0B 2025 guidance. Recovery dependent on low-carbon capex restart + ethylene cycle. Watch Q1-Q2 2026 awards as the inflection.
