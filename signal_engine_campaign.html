Section 01
Pipeline architecture
Seven stages. The first four are deterministic — rule-based, no AI, fast, cheap, and auditable. Stage five is the Claude classification. Stages six and seven are mechanical aggregation and write. Claude touches only the signals that survive stages one through four. This is not optional. Sending 1,500 raw signals to Claude is expensive, slow, and introduces noise. The pre-filter stages exist to protect the quality of the classification.

Section 01B — Foundational principle
The Daily Context Document
The system is stateless by design. Every API call to Claude is a new session. Claude does not remember yesterday's run. This is not a limitation — it is the consistency guarantee. Drift, habit, and accumulated bias cannot enter a stateless call. The same prompt, the same model version, the same rules produce the same class of output every day. That is auditable. A persistent conversation is not.

But stateless does not mean context-free. Context lives in the database, not in the conversation. The Daily Context Document is how structured intelligence — hard state from the database and soft learning from the analyst — enters the P1 prompt each morning without compromising consistency. Claude does not learn. The document gets richer. Day 500's classification is better than day 1's because the document injected into the prompt is more informative — not because Claude has developed opinions.
Stateless by design — non-negotiable WF-15 makes a fresh HTTP POST to the Anthropic API on every 6am run. No session ID is reused. No conversation history is sent. The Daily Context Document is the only mechanism by which prior intelligence enters the classification prompt. Any proposal to use conversation continuity or persistent sessions for "better context" must be rejected. It trades auditability for the illusion of memory.

v1.1 amendment — COMMENTARY split COMMENTARY is replaced by two distinct types. GENERAL_COMMENTARY produces delta = 0 on every hypothesis — no exceptions, same rule as before. DIRECTIONAL_COMMENTARY may apply a maximum confidence_score adjustment of ±2 — to confidence only, never to probability. It cannot advance a step, cannot satisfy a falsifier, and cannot be the sole basis for any probability change. The post-processing code validates both: any GENERAL_COMMENTARY returning non-zero is rejected; any DIRECTIONAL_COMMENTARY returning a probability delta (as opposed to confidence_adjustment) is rejected. Rationale: early directional intelligence in analysis and forecasts is real signal. A zero cap discards it entirely. A ±2 confidence nudge preserves it without creating probability drift untethered from hard evidence.
How to distinguish DIRECTIONAL from GENERAL. The test is specificity of mechanism. "Analysts expect hydrogen to grow" = GENERAL_COMMENTARY. "BloombergNEF revises electrolyser cost curve down 18% citing scale effects from Chinese manufacturing" = DIRECTIONAL_COMMENTARY. The second names a mechanism, a magnitude, and an implied threshold. That earns the soft lane.

Two prompts. Two distinct jobs. These are locked assets. They govern every classification the system makes and every email it produces. Version any change. Record the reason. Date it. The prompts are the intellectual core of the system — treat them accordingly.

You are the classification engine of the FutureBridge Signal Engine. Your job is precise, structured, and governed by strict rules. You do not summarise. You do not explain. You classify. You receive a list of pre-screened signals and a structured hypothesis register. For each signal, you must determine: 1. Which hypotheses it provides direct evidence for or against 2. The probability delta for each matched hypothesis — capped by the signal type supplied 3. The direction of the evidence (confirming or refuting) 4. A one-sentence evidence statement, factual and specific, citing the signal RULES YOU MUST NEVER BREAK: R1 — Signal type cap is absolute. The signal_type field carries a max_delta value. You cannot return a delta larger than that value in absolute terms. A GENERAL_COMMENTARY signal has max_delta 0 — return delta 0. A DIRECTIONAL_COMMENTARY signal has max_delta ±2 on confidence_score only — never on probability. No exceptions. R2 — Evidence must be direct. Only match a hypothesis if the signal directly evidences one of its stated step conditions, falsifiers, or core claim. Do not infer. Do not extrapolate. If the connection requires more than one logical step, do not match. R3 — Uncertainty means zero. If you are uncertain whether a signal evidences a hypothesis, return delta 0 and reason "insufficient direct evidence". Do not guess. Do not split the difference with a small delta. R4 — One signal can move multiple hypotheses. A single signal may match several hypotheses. Each match is evaluated independently. Each gets its own delta, capped by the signal type. R5 — Step advancement requires explicit evidence. A hypothesis step is marked as advanced only if the signal directly satisfies the step condition as written. Partial evidence does not advance a step. R6 — Output is JSON only. No preamble. No explanation outside the JSON. No markdown. If you cannot produce valid JSON, return the error structure defined below. R7 — DIRECTIONAL_COMMENTARY is confidence-only. When signal_type is DIRECTIONAL_COMMENTARY, you may return a confidence_adjustment of ±1 or ±2 against matched hypotheses. Set delta = 0 and step_advanced = false. Section 06
Aggregation & scoring
After Claude returns classifications, four scores are computed mechanically — no AI. These scores determine which signals surface in v16 and which get written to the heat map.
B
Breadth score
Count of distinct hypotheses touched by this signal. A signal touching 8 hypotheses scores higher breadth than one touching 1, regardless of delta magnitude.
D
Depth score
Average absolute delta across all hypothesis matches. A signal with 2 matches of ±12 scores higher depth than one with 10 matches of ±2.
R
Topic relevance
1–10. Proportion of matched hypotheses that carry today's topic_code tag. Signals relevant to today's campaign topic rank higher in v16.
N
Novelty score
How different is this signal from the recent signal distribution for matched hypotheses? Reversal signals and step advances score highest. Trend confirmation for a hypothesis that moved three times this month scores lowest. See Section 06B.
Composite formula. Signal score = (B × 0.25) + (D × 0.35) + (R × 0.20) + (N × 0.20). Maximum 10. Scores are computed for every classified signal. The quality gate in Section 06B determines whether any email is generated at all.
Section 06B — Quality gate
Signal quality gate & audience precision
The system has no production obligation. A quiet day produces no output. That is not a failure — it is the correct answer. This section defines the minimum quality threshold a signal must clear before any email is generated, how novelty is calculated, and how audience size is derived from the signal itself rather than fixed at a volume target.
The null output principle If no signal clears the quality threshold, WF-15 produces a run summary only: best signal score, threshold, gap, and a one-line description of why no signal cleared. No YAMM CSV. No email. The analyst receives the summary. A quiet market correctly produces silence. An intelligence service that always finds something to say is not an intelligence service.
N
Novelty score — definition and calculation
Computed mechanically from Datasette signal history · No AI · 0–10 scale
Deterministic No AI

Novelty measures how different this signal is from the recent signal distribution for the hypotheses it touches. A signal confirming a trend that has already moved a hypothesis four times this month is low novelty. A signal that reverses direction, advances a step, or introduces a signal type not seen in 30 days is high novelty. This dimension is what separates resonant intelligence from incremental confirmation.

ConditionNovelty pointsRationale
Signal advances a hypothesis step+10 (maximum)Step advances are the rarest, highest-value events in the system. Always high novelty.
Signal reverses current velocity direction on matched hypothesis+8A reversal contradicts the established trend. Executives who already know the trend need the reversal, not the confirmation.
Signal type not seen on this hypothesis in 30 days+6A new signal type arriving on a long-static hypothesis is an early indicator of state change.

Novelty is per-signal, not per-hypothesis. If a signal touches five hypotheses with different novelty conditions, the signal's novelty score is the maximum novelty across all matches — not the average. One step advance is sufficient to make the whole signal high novelty regardless of what the other matches score.
DCD dependency. Novelty calculation requires the last 30 days of signal history per hypothesis. This is part of the hard state layer in the Daily Context Document — specifically the signal type history and step history fields. If the DCD hard state is incomplete for a hypothesis, novelty defaults to +2 for that match. The system does not guess.

Section 09
Audit rules
The system's value as a consulting methodology depends on its auditability. Every probability change must be traceable to a signal. Every signal must be traceable to a source. These rules are not aspirational — they are enforced in the code.
  • No probability update without a signal_id. The Apps Script POST action probability_update rejects any payload missing signal_id. The field is required, not optional.
  • No step advancement without analyst confirmation. Claude can flag step_advanced: true. WF-15 writes this to the Datasette signals table. It does not automatically advance the step in the Sheet. Step advancement is written to the Sheet only when the analyst confirms it in v16 or the audit tool.
  • Probability is bounded 1–99. No hypothesis reaches 0 or 100. Those values imply certainty. The system does not express certainty.
  • Every run is logged. The run_id field links every signal, every classification, and every Sheet update to a specific 6am run. If a run produces erroneous updates, they can be identified and reversed by run_id.
  • Classification errors are logged, not silently dropped. Any signal failing post-processing validation is written to an errors table in Datasette with the violation type and the rejected value. Errors are visible in the audit tool.
  • Prompt versions are recorded. Each classification run records the prompt version used (P1 v1.1, P2-NEWS v1.1 etc.). If a prompt is amended, the version increments and all subsequent runs carry the new version identifier. Runs executed under prior versions are identifiable by their version tag — do not retroactively reprocess them.
  • The analyst is the last gate. No email reaches a contact without the analyst opening v16, reviewing the email previews, and clicking Download. The system generates. The analyst approves.
Section 11 — Hypothesis lifecycle

A hypothesis register that only grows is a liability. Hypotheses that were right in 2026 can be quietly wrong by 2027. Section 12 — Strategy reality
The stated vs revealed strategy layer
A hypothesis anchored to what a company says is PR analysis. A hypothesis anchored to what a company does is strategy intelligence. These are rarely the same thing. Shell's sustainability report says energy transition. Shell's capex allocation says LNG. Validating hypotheses against the website produces confidently wrong analysis. This section defines the mechanism that separates narrative from behaviour — and makes the divergence between them a named, auditable signal type.
The core rule Every hypothesis that names a company as a decision actor must reference that company's revealed strategy — not its stated strategy. A hypothesis where the only supporting evidence is a company's public narrative is flagged DIVERGENT until a hard commercial signal confirms behavioural alignment. The system tracks what companies do, not what they say they believe.

Schema change — v4.1 DCD soft context

When a hypothesis names a company as a decision actor, and that company's revealed strategy contradicts the hypothesis direction, the hypothesis carries a strategy_divergence flag. This flag enters the DCD soft context layer — Claude reads it every morning before classifying signals. It changes how signals from that entity are weighted against that hypothesis.

Flag valueMeaningDCD instruction to Claude
ALIGNED Revealed strategy is consistent with hypothesis direction. Company is acting in the way the hypothesis predicts. No modification to signal weighting. Standard classification rules apply.
PARTIAL Some revealed evidence supports the hypothesis. Other revealed actions suggest hedging or mixed commitment. "Treat signals from [entity] on [hypothesis] as moderate evidence. Require commercial contract or FID — announcements alone insufficient."
DIVERGENT Revealed strategy contradicts the hypothesis direction. The company is spending money in the opposite direction from what the hypothesis predicts. "Shell's revealed capex contradicts BET_E004. Weight Shell signals against this hypothesis as ambiguous until a hard commercial contract appears. Announcements and partnerships do not move probability."
How the flag enters the DCD. WF-16 checks strategy_divergence on every active hypothesis daily. If a hypothesis carries DIVERGENT or PARTIAL and has no corresponding DCD soft context entry, WF-16 proposes a DCD entry for analyst review. The entry is standardised: entity name, hypothesis ID, divergence rating, divergence evidence sentence, and the specific instruction for Claude. The analyst accepts or edits — the accepted version enters tomorrow's DCD.
Divergence is not a reason to retire a hypothesis. A hypothesis that Shell will pivot from LNG to hydrogen by 2030 may be correct even though Shell's current capex says LNG. The divergence flag changes how signals are weighted — it does not invalidate the hypothesis. A company whose revealed strategy contradicts the hypothesis direction and then signs a commercial contract in that direction is a high-novelty signal. The system should detect that reversal, not suppress it.

Section 13 — v1.6 · Business-first architecture
The board-first hypothesis — schema v4.2
Boards do not care about technology. They care about decisions — what to commit, when to commit, what they lose if they wait. The hypothesis must lead with the business decision and carry the system drivers underneath it as the evidence layer. Technology is one driver of five. It is rarely the primary one in energy and mobility. Schema v4.2 restructures the hypothesis around this reality.
The fundamental inversion — v4.2 Prior schema: hypothesis is a technical or market claim, decision is attached. v4.2: hypothesis is a business decision claim, system drivers are the evidence layer underneath. The title field must use board language. Technical language is confined to WNTBT conditions. A hypothesis whose title reads like a technology briefing is structurally wrong and will produce the wrong email, the wrong audience, and the wrong client conversation.
