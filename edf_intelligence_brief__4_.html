EDF is the world's largest nuclear power company and France's dominant energy utility. Renationalised in 2023 by the French state, EDF is now executing a nuclear renaissance strategy — recovering production from its 2022 nadir (corrosion issues) and building 6 new EPR2 reactors. This places EDF at the centre of the global advanced nuclear conversation: SMRs, large-scale EPR, and nuclear as the backbone of a clean electricity system. FutureBridge's H3 intelligence on advanced nuclear, SMRs, and nuclear-hydrogen integration is highly relevant.
02

Strategy & Intelligence

Strategic Assessment: The global nuclear renaissance is EDF's strategic tailwind. With AI data centres demanding always-on clean power and European energy security concerns elevated post-Ukraine, nuclear is back in vogue. The FutureBridge angle: EDF needs technology intelligence on advanced reactor designs, SMR competitive landscape, and nuclear-hydrogen integration pathways — all H2/H3 horizon questions.
Nuclear Fleet Recovery & Extension
  • French fleet of 56 reactors: recovering from 2022 corrosion (SCC) issues — 360+ TWh targeted 2024
  • Grand Carénage: €50B+ life extension programme for existing fleet to 2035-2040
  • New EPR2 programme: 6 reactors announced (Penly, Gravelines sites) — 2037-2038 first power
  • Flamanville EPR: finally approaching commercial operation (delayed by 15+ years)
  • UK Hinkley Point C: under construction in Somerset; £33B project — 3.2 GW
Advanced Nuclear & New Technologies
  • SMR (Small Modular Reactors): EDF developing NUWARD SMR concept (170 MW each) for 2030s deployment
  • Framatome: nuclear engineering arm with global fuel supply and reactor services capability
  • Nuclear hydrogen: exploring coupling of nuclear heat/power to electrolysis for clean H₂
  • Nuclear-powered district heating: using reactor waste heat for French cities — Caen pilot
  • Advanced nuclear fuels: Framatome developing accident-tolerant fuels (ATF) for improved safety
The Renationalisation Context
  • EDF was renationalised in 2023 — removing investor pressure but adding political direction
  • French government sees nuclear as a sovereignty issue — EDF is executing state policy, not just commercial strategy
  • This creates unusual dynamics: EDF is a near-infinite capital patient investor in nuclear
  • But: EDF's debt load (€60B+) is a constraint — needs electricity price recovery for FCF generation
  • FutureBridge positioning: treat EDF as a quasi-government client; relationship at senior advisory level
Global Nuclear Export Ambition
  • EDF-Framatome is competing for nuclear contracts in UK (Sizewell C), India, Poland, Finland
  • Competing against Korean (KEPCO), US (Westinghouse), Russian (Rosatom) and Chinese (CGN) vendors
  • FutureBridge can support EDF's international BD with country-specific energy policy intelligence
  • The SMR race is heating up — EDF/NUWARD vs Rolls-Royce SMR vs NuScale vs others
  • Whoever wins the European SMR standard could dominate a multi-trillion dollar global market

Key Initiatives & Progress

EPR2 New Build Programme
ANNOUNCED
6 new EPR2 reactors at Penly and Gravelines sites. First power ~2037-2038. Total cost €50B+. Represents France's largest industrial programme since original nuclear fleet. Critical for French energy sovereignty.
Progress15%
Flamanville EPR — Commercial Operation
IMMINENT
The long-delayed 1.6 GW EPR is finally approaching commercial operation after 15+ years and cost overruns from €3.3B to ~€13B. Lessons learned are informing EPR2 design improvements.
Progress90%
NUWARD SMR Development
DEVELOPING
EDF's 170 MW SMR design. French government backing. Targeting 2030s deployment. Competing in European SMR tender landscape vs Rolls-Royce (UK), NuScale (US). Design validation phase.
Progress25%
Grand Carénage — Fleet Life Extension
ONGOING
€50B+ programme to extend 32 reactors from 40-year to 50-60 year operating life. Ongoing across French fleet. Core near-term cash flow protection strategy.
Progress60%
Nuclear-Powered District Heating
PILOT
Caen pilot project: coupling existing reactor waste heat to district heating network. Could decarbonise urban heating across French cities using existing nuclear assets. Technically proven; scaling is the question.
Progress20%
Hinkley Point C — UK Construction
BUILDING
3.2 GW nuclear plant under construction in Somerset, UK. £33B project. EDF 66.5% stake; CGN 33.5%. First UK nuclear in decades. Target: 2028-2031 first power (subject to further delays).
Progress50%
