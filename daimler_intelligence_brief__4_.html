Mercedes-Benz has made the clearest strategic choice among premium OEMs: fewer, more expensive, more profitable vehicles. CEO Ola Källenius is pursuing a 'top-end' strategy — reducing volume while increasing profitability per unit. The EQS and EQE represent genuine luxury EV technology leadership. MB.OS (in-house operating system) is the software-defined vehicle bet. The risk: China JV profits (50% of group) under pressure from local competitors.
02

Strategy & Intelligence

Strategic Assessment: Mercedes' intelligence need is about luxury differentiation in the EV era: what technology and experience innovations will justify premium pricing when battery range, performance and features are democratising? FutureBridge's H2/H3 scanning on luxury materials, autonomous driving, and premium in-vehicle experience is highly relevant.
Top-End Luxury EV Leadership
  • EQS: world's most technologically advanced luxury EV — 800+ km range, Hyperscreen, Level 3 autonomy
  • EQE: premium executive EV competing with BMW i5 and Audi A6 e-tron
  • AMG EQ: performance EV sub-brand — maintaining AMG brand equity in electric era
  • Maybach EQS: ultra-luxury EV reaching customers who would otherwise buy Rolls-Royce
  • G-Class Electric: legendary off-roader going electric — critical brand heritage in EV format
MB.OS — The Software Platform Bet
  • MB.OS: in-house automotive operating system — MBUX HyperScreen is the showroom version
  • Over-the-air updates: revenue stream for continuous vehicle improvement post-purchase
  • Level 3 autonomy: Mercedes Drive Pilot — legally approved in Germany and Nevada; world-first
  • Partnership with Google: Maps, Assistant integration while MB.OS handles core functions
  • Data monetisation: in-vehicle data for personalisation, targeted services, fleet management
China: The 50% Problem
  • Beijing Benz (BBDC JV) historically contributed 50%+ of group profits — now under severe pressure
  • Chinese luxury buyers increasingly choosing local brands: Lixiang, NIO ES8, ZEEKR 009
  • Mercedes China response: fully locally developed EV models at Chinese price points — in development
  • G-Class and Maybach still strong in China (status symbols) — but mainstream EQE/EQS weakening
  • FutureBridge: Chinese ultra-premium mobility preferences and ZEEKR/NIO competitive intelligence
FutureBridge Entry Points
  • Mercedes-Benz Research & Development: large R&D organisation scanning materials, powertrain, sensors
  • MBtech / Mercedes Advanced Design: future vehicle concept development
  • MBUX and digital experience team: in-vehicle software and customer experience intelligence
  • Sustainability team: lifecycle emissions, circular economy materials, sustainable luxury
  • China strategy: separate team managing the JV and Chinese market intelligence needs
