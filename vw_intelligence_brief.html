The VW core brand is the epicentre of the Volkswagen Group's existential crisis. Having committed to an aggressive EV transition that has not generated expected volumes, while losing market share in China to BYD and local EV brands, and facing structurally uncompetitive German manufacturing costs, VW brand CEO Thomas Schäfer announced factory closures and 35,000 job cuts in 2024 — unprecedented for post-war German industry. The brand is in a survival restructuring while simultaneously trying to execute an EV ramp.
02

Strategy & Intelligence

Strategic Assessment: VW brand's challenge is fundamentally about cost structure and speed. FutureBridge's value is in technology intelligence on Chinese EV manufacturing processes, battery technology cost trajectories, and software-defined vehicle architectures — the three areas where VW is most exposed relative to BYD and Tesla.
Cost Restructuring — The Burning Platform
  • German plant closures: first in VW's history — Emden and/or Osnabrück under review
  • €5.7B annual cost savings target: labour, manufacturing overhead, complexity reduction
  • Platform consolidation: reducing from 40+ platforms across group to fewer shared architectures
  • Supplier renegotiations: demanding 10%+ cost reductions from tier 1 and 2 suppliers
  • Software: VW.OS development costs being restructured; CARIAD (software arm) being refocused
EV Repositioning
  • ID. family: ID.3, ID.4, ID.5, ID.7 — all on MEB platform; strong product but insufficient volumes
  • SSP (Scalable Systems Platform): next-generation EV architecture; delayed but strategic
  • Battery: joint venture with PowerCo (VW Group battery subsidiary) for cell production
  • China: launching local joint venture EVs with SAIC; competing on cost vs BYD
  • Software-defined vehicle: targeting OTA revenue, in-vehicle services, Level 3 autonomy by 2025
China Is the Existential Variable
  • VW built its global success on China — 40% of group profit historically came from China JVs
  • BYD and Chinese NEV brands have captured the EV segment VW expected to dominate
  • VW's China response: local EV models with SAIC at BYD price points — but speed is the question
  • If China recovers, VW's restructuring is manageable. If China deteriorates further, it's existential.
  • FutureBridge intelligence on Chinese EV technology roadmaps is directly applicable to VW's China strategy team
FutureBridge Angle — Technology Intelligence for Survival
  • VW Group Innovation: actively scanning battery chemistry, solid-state, manufacturing automation
  • The question isn't whether to electrify — it's HOW to do it faster and at lower cost than current trajectory
  • Chinese manufacturing process intelligence is a sensitive but critical need
  • Software-defined vehicle intelligence: how Tesla and Chinese OEMs are monetising vehicle software
  • FutureBridge can position as the 'competitive intelligence' layer for VW's technology strategy
