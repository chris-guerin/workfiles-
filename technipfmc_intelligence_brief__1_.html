Strategy & Intelligence

Subsea super-cycle ride
  • $16.8B backlog with execution phasing favouring 2026-2027 ($5.7B + $8.6B respectively).
  • Q3 2025 EBITDA +36% YoY — Subsea segment driving most of the lift (Investing.com Q3 2025 slides note).
  • iEPCI integrated direct model drove sequential growth in Q3 2025 — Africa, Americas, Australia activity.
  • Brazil + Guyana are the structural growth corridors via Petrobras and ExxonMobil flagship contracts.
Customer lock-in: Petrobras + ExxonMobil
  • Petrobras subsea production systems contract — multi-year frame for greenfield + brownfield + asset revitalisation; covers installation support + life-of-field services.
  • ExxonMobil Guyana — 7/7 awards since first contract in 2017, including Hammerhead (Q3 2025 — the 7th award). Effective exclusivity on Guyana subsea.
  • iEPCI vs traditional EPCI — direct-access integration model widens the addressable economic spread vs Subsea7, Saipem, McDermott.
2026 financial guidance — first formal disclosure
  • $10B subsea inbound orders 2026 (vs Q3 2025 inbound $2.4B annualised ≈ $9.6B, so guidance roughly matches run-rate).
  • $531M adjusted EBITDA ex-FX 2026.
  • $448M free cash flow 2026.
  • "Higher economic returns and greater project certainty" per Q3 2025 — operator FIDs underwritten by oil prices that pencil deepwater.
Where FutureBridge can engage
  • iEPCI vs traditional EPCI competitive positioning intelligence — Subsea7, Saipem, McDermott economics.
  • Guyana exclusivity defensibility — 8th + 9th ExxonMobil awards as the test.
  • Petrobras frame contract execution + revitalisation pipeline mapping.
