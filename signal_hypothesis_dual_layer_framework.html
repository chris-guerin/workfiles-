Signal Engine — Dual Hypothesis Architecture

This document separates signal hypotheses from consulting hypotheses. They may share a name and a forward-looking direction, but they do not carry the same depth, payload, or job. One exists to turn noise into signal and make future decisions visible. The other exists to support a project, a client decision, and a deeper consulting frame built from landscape, ecosystem, and programme-level detail.

Core point. A signal engine does not need the full depth of a consulting hypothesis. It needs a lighter hypothesis object that is good enough to filter news, map driver movement, and make future decision windows visible. Section 01

Core logic — what the system is actually doing

The system is not just collecting news. It is turning high-volume external noise into structured signals, attaching those signals to lightweight future-facing hypotheses, and making movement in the future visible early enough to act on it. That is the daily intelligence layer. A project then takes a selected theme and deepens it into a consulting-grade hypothesis supported by wider evidence, market structure, ecosystem, economics, and execution logic.

Section 02

Schematic — how the layers relate

Input layer

External inputs

Raw material: news, patents, papers, earnings, regulation, company announcements, market signals, funding, partnerships, technical milestones.

Problem: high volume, duplication, weak context, too much noise, not decision-ready.

Job: convert noise into structured signal objects.

Signal hypothesis layer

Lightweight future-facing hypotheses

Purpose: filter, classify, and interpret signals.

Depth: light.

Question: did the world move in a way that changes the likelihood, speed, or shape of this future?

Consulting hypothesis layer

Project-grade hypotheses

Purpose: support business decisions.

Depth: heavy.

Question: what does this mean for a client, a market, a strategy, a capability, an investment, or a programme?

Section 03

Two hypothesis layers — same name, different payload

Signal hypothesis

Used by the signal engine

Purpose: attach a signal to a future-facing frame.

  • Light enough for daily automated use
  • Built around a small number of drivers
  • Good enough to detect threshold movement
  • Designed for ranking, filtering, and probability shifts
  • Does not need full market proof
Example signal hypothesis Sodium-ion batteries become viable in stationary storage before 2030. Key drivers: - cell cost moves below threshold - cycle life becomes acceptable - Chinese scale-up becomes real - LFP economics become less dominant
same forward direction
different depth
Consulting hypothesis

Used by a project team

Purpose: frame an advisory case and support action.

  • Built with much deeper evidence
  • Needs market landscape and ecosystem structure
  • Needs economics, capabilities, competitors, timelines
  • May include customer adoption and geography
  • Supports strategic choices, portfolio moves, and programme design
Example consulting hypothesis Sodium-ion batteries will capture a defined share of global stationary storage in cost-sensitive balancing and duration segments, with specific implications for chemistry strategy, supply chain positioning, and regional manufacturing footprint.
Section 04

Inputs to hypotheses — what moves them

A signal hypothesis should not try to carry the whole consulting case. It should hold enough structure to let incoming signals move the right driver. That means the hypothesis should be fed by clean external inputs and interpreted through a small number of meaningful drivers.

Input types

News, regulation, patents, funding, technical milestones, supply chain commitments, plant announcements, contracts, standards, policy changes, strategic moves.

Signal drivers

Cost thresholds, performance thresholds, commercial commitments, ecosystem build-out, regulation, geography, critical actors, technical bottlenecks.

Consulting deepeners

Landscape size, competitor map, customer need, value chain, margin logic, capability gaps, partner set, adoption logic, investment windows.

Input What it means in the signal engine What it becomes in a project
News event A possible movement in one driver Evidence point inside a wider trend and market story
Patent / paper Possible technical feasibility movement Technology landscape, IP position, capability depth
Regulation Constraint or accelerator on hypothesis timing Commercial viability, compliance logic, market opening
Plant / scale-up Commercial seriousness and ecosystem proof Supply chain map, actor positioning, who may win
Contract / offtake Threshold event or de-risking move Business case proof, adoption proof, customer readiness
Section 05

Project deepening — where the consulting work gets much deeper

Once a signal hypothesis starts to matter, the work changes. The question is no longer just “did the world move?” It becomes “what does this mean for a client, a market, a portfolio, a capability, or a programme?” That is where consulting depth comes in.

Daily intelligence layer

What the signal engine is trying to achieve

  • Reduce noise to signal
  • Map signal to the right hypothesis
  • Track driver movement over time
  • Make future decision windows visible early
  • Flag where the existing hypothesis register is weak
Project / consulting layer

What the consulting project is trying to achieve

  • Build a defensible view of the market or opportunity
  • Landscape the players, technologies, geographies, and value chain
  • Map the ecosystem and dependencies
  • Build scenarios, economics, and decision logic
  • Translate external movement into a client action frame

Landscape

What exists, who is active, what technologies are credible, how the market is segmented, where the weak signals are clustering, and which areas are over- or under-served.

Ecosystem

Who enables the market, who de-risks it, which suppliers, partners, channels, regulators, investors, customers, and standards bodies matter, and where dependency sits.

Economics and viability

Cost curves, margin logic, incentives, capex, opex, threshold points, commercial structures, speed to viability, and what needs to be true for adoption or scale.

Projects and programmes

What should be trialled, what should be tracked, where capability needs to be built, which partnerships matter, and which investment or go-to-market decisions become visible.

Section 06

Working rule — what each layer should and should not do

Layer Should do Should not do
Signal hypothesis Filter, classify, rank, detect movement, surface embryo hypotheses, make future decisions visible Carry the full market model, project logic, or full business case
Consulting hypothesis Frame the strategic case, support projects, integrate landscape and ecosystem depth, support choices Be used as a daily filter object inside a lightweight signal loop
Signal archive Store structured evidence and movement over time Pretend that one daily winner is enough intelligence
Project work Deepen the hypothesis and make it client-useful Rebuild the detection engine every time a project starts
