Brand Architecture Review
VW Group rethinking Audi's role vs Porsche
Audi occupies a complex position within VW Group: it is the group's primary profit engine for premium vehicles, yet it faces the same structural challenges — Chinese competition, software lag, and EV volume shortfall — that afflict the broader group. CEO Gernot Döllner is executing a difficult dual mandate: deep cost restructuring (18,000 German jobs) while simultaneously ramping the PPE platform (e-tron GT, A6 e-tron) to compete with BMW iX and Mercedes EQ. The stakes are enormous: Audi's profitability cross-subsidises much of the VW Group's EV investment.

Strategy & Intelligence

Strategic Assessment: Audi's challenge is premium EV positioning: how to maintain brand premium and margin vs BMW and Mercedes while also defending against Chinese premium disruptors (NIO, Li Auto, ZEEKR). FutureBridge's value is in technology intelligence on premium EV differentiation — software experience, battery technology, autonomy, and interior innovation.
PPE Platform — Premium EV Foundation
  • Premium Platform Electric (PPE): joint development with Porsche — 800V architecture, ultra-fast charging
  • e-tron GT (Audi) / Taycan (Porsche): 800V, 270kW charging, best-in-class charging speed
  • A6 e-tron (2024): PPE's first high-volume model — competing with BMW i5, Mercedes EQE
  • Q6 e-tron: PPE SUV version — critical for volume; competing with BMW iX3, Merc EQC
  • ARTEMIS: advanced autonomy and software team developing next-gen digital experience
Brand Premium Defence
  • Quattro: all-wheel drive brand equity — being translated to EV with torque vectoring systems
  • RS performance: RS e-tron GT — maintaining performance brand credentials in EV era
  • Design language: premium interior materials and design as key differentiation vs software-led competitors
  • Lamborghini electrification: Urus PHEV launched; full EV Lamborghini 2028
  • Ducati electric motorcycle: entry into electric two-wheelers with brand premium
China Is Critical — And Challenging
  • China historically generated 35%+ of Audi's global profits — now under severe pressure
  • NIO's ET9, Li Auto L9, ZEEKR 001 are directly competing with Audi's premium positioning at lower prices
  • Chinese consumers are increasingly choosing Chinese premium brands over European imports
  • Audi's response: FAW-Volkswagen JV developing China-specific premium EVs (AUDI brand, different from global)
  • Speed of China-specific EV launch is the critical variable for Audi's 2025-2027 earnings
Software Experience as Differentiator
  • Audi's MMI infotainment has historically been a weakness vs BMW iDrive and Mercedes MBUX
  • ARTEMIS team developing next-gen software experience — OTA, AI assistant, autonomous features
  • Partnership discussions with tech companies for in-vehicle AI (Google, potentially others)
  • FutureBridge intelligence: how are Chinese premium OEMs monetising software and services in-vehicle?
  • The software gap vs NIO and ZEEKR is Audi's most urgent technology intelligence need
