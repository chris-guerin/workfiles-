01 — The problem

Intelligence without action is just noise.

FutureBridge tracks thousands of weak signals. None of that mattered if it didn't reach the right person, at the right moment, with the right commercial argument.

The gap wasn't research quality. It was the distance between an analyst identifying a signal and a client decision-maker receiving something they'd actually act on. That distance was manual, slow, and inconsistent.

  • Signal detection was decoupled from outreach. Analysts found things. No one systematically connected findings to contact-level action.
  • Outreach was generic. Emails that lead with firm credentials don't get replies. Emails that lead with a specific signal, its commercial consequence, and a pointed question do.
  • Scale was the constraint. Good, personalised, commercially grounded outreach takes time. Time is what we don't have at 173 contacts across 93 companies.
  • Hypotheses were implicit. Every analyst carries assumptions about where markets are going. None of them were structured, scored, or updated.

04 — The pipeline

Automated. Every weekday. 07:00.

WF-15 is the classification engine. It ingests new signals, evaluates them against the hypothesis repository using a structured reasoning chain, updates probability scores, and flags high-relevance hits for outreach. No analyst involvement required at the classification stage.

01
Signal ingestion
New signals enter via webhook or manual trigger. Each carries source, date, sector tag, and raw text. n8n routes to WF-15.
Automated
02
Hypothesis matching
Claude API loads the relevant hypotheses from the repository. Load-bearing check first — does this signal move a falsifier or trigger condition? Trigger proximity second.
Automated
03
Probability update
Matched hypotheses receive a probability delta. Confidence and urgency scores update. Changes write back to the Google Sheets repository via Apps Script endpoint.
Automated
04
Contact resolution
High-relevance hits trigger a contact lookup against the Datasette database. Contacts are matched by company, sector, and seniority. Role-specific consequence framing is loaded.
Automated
05
Email generation
Four-beat outreach protocol: specific signal with real numbers → strategic posture of the recipient's company → business-level consequence for their role → pointed question + CTA. HubSpot {{first_name}} preserved throughout.
Automated
06
HubSpot push
Generated emails push to HubSpot CRM via WF-12 API. Contacts tagged, sequence enrolled. Analyst reviews before send — this is the only manual gate.

05 — What it produces

Six types of output. One consistent standard.

OUT — 01
Personalised outreach emails
Role-specific, signal-led, commercially grounded. No generic intros. No firm credentials in the body copy. The firm name never appears. Written to prompt a reply from a decision-maker who gets a hundred emails a week.
OUT — 02
Meeting intelligence briefs
Auto-generated pre-meeting briefs covering the contact's recent activity, strategic focus, and commercial pressure points. Questions are structured so each answer maps to a specific FutureBridge sale.
OUT — 03
Client intelligence dashboards
16 accounts covered. Dark editorial format, hosted on GitHub Pages. Each brief includes a live AI Refresh Button that calls the Anthropic API to update weak signals in real time, plus a multi-turn Ask AI panel.
OUT — 04
Hypothesis probability updates
Every signal that passes classification writes a delta back to the repository. Over time, the probability scores become a live, evidence-based view of where each market is heading — not a static analyst opinion.
OUT — 05
Campaign wave packages
Segmented outreach campaigns with sector-specific email variants. Energy, PE, Mobility. Each campaign wave carries a consistent signal thesis, differentiated by recipient persona: C-Suite, R&D, Strategy, HR.
OUT — 06
Geopolitical impact intelligence
Real-time conflict monitoring mapped to FutureBridge's six sectors. A 2×2 commercial priority matrix distinguishes clients under stress from those with competitive advantage from geopolitical disruption.
